## Q1 — Posthumous / time-delayed delivery (GoodTrust, SafeBeyond, Evaheld) + family expectations
**Headline:** The category's failure mode is now *evidenced*, not theoretical — SafeBeyond
(funded, insurer-partnered posthumous-messaging flagship) is **defunct as of 2026** and
Legacy Locker "went dark" post-acquisition. The decades promise breaks by **vendor
disappearance, not breach.**

- **GoodTrust (live 2026)** — automated dead-man's-switch (1–4 check-ins/period; 3 missed
  replies fires), Future Messages by email/social + assigned-contact access. Inactivity
  proxy ≠ verified death. Founded 2020 (R. Steiber), ~$7.5–8.4M raised, NerdWallet #1
  2024 & 2025 only. *(official + analysis)*
- **SafeBeyond — DEFUNCT** (ceased ~July 2022; site unreachable June 2026, reproduced).
  Funded ~$1.1M, Munich Re partner, gone in ~8 yrs. *(synthesis live-fetch + analysis)*
- **Evaheld (live 2026; new competitor)** — human-coordinator trigger, bundles advance-care
  planning, posthumous gated to paid tier, **no encryption standard disclosed**. *(official + analysis)*
- **Families expect** both emotional presence *and* practical continuity, kept separate
  *(analysis — vendor blog, single-source; corroborate independently)*.
- **Resolved:** prior open item "no confirmed shutdown-with-data-loss case" — now evidenced twice
  (SafeBeyond, Legacy Locker; ~26 defunct per The Digital Beyond).
- **Refuted & excluded:** the "double-edged grief / 'inestimable comfort' / anniversary
  algorithmic-resurfacing" claim — NOT in the cited IE School article; re-source from
  Brubaker et al. / continuing-bonds literature.

## Q2 — Durability & data-loss-prevention standards for decades-scale trust
**Headline:** Decades trust is earned against a **named preservation-standards stack
(OAIS / NDSA / PREMIS / LOCKSS / format normalization)**, not cloud uptime — and the single
highest-leverage feature is a standards-based, family-controlled **export**, because the
dominant decades risk is operator error and vendor death, not disk failure.

- **OAIS (ISO 14721, 2025)** — six functions incl. standing **Preservation Planning**;
  turns storage into an archive. *(official)*
- **NDSA Levels (2019)** — 4 levels × 5 areas (Storage, Fixity, Security, Metadata, Formats);
  the maturity self-assessment a product should score against. *(official)*
- **Fixity = checksum at ingest + PERIODIC re-verification** (not upload-time only). *(official)*
- **PREMIS v3.0** records provenance/fixity/chain-of-custody. **Correction:** PREMIS ≠ ISO 16363
  (sibling standards, not "adjacent"). *(official)*
- **Format normalization/migration** to open lossless masters (TIFF/JPEG2000/DNG, WAV, PDF/A);
  HEIC/Live-Photo/current codecs may not render in 30 yrs. *(official)*
- **LOCKSS** — "the threat is ourselves"; diverse mutually-distrusting custody. *(official)*
- **AWS S3 11-nines is scoped to device/AZ loss** — versioning + Object Lock for user/app error. *(official)*
- **Synthesis lesson added:** highest-leverage durability feature = standards-based,
  family-controlled full export (open masters + fixity manifest).

## Q3 — Privacy circles & consent (child-safety, GDPR/PII)
**Headline:** Competitors converge on owner-controlled, invite-only, parent-as-proxy circles —
but the binding child-data floor is a **TWO-regime stack (US COPPA under-13 + EU GDPR 13–16 +
UK Children's Code defaults)**, and "keep forever" now collides with COPPA's new
anti-indefinite-retention rule.

- **COPPA (US, under-13)** runs parallel to GDPR Art.8 — satisfy **both**. 2025 amendments
  (eff. 21 Jun 2025): **separate** consent for third-party disclosure; **prohibit indefinite
  retention** w/o a stated purpose; biometrics now PII. *(official + analysis)*
- **UK Children's Code** — 15 design-level standards (high-privacy defaults, geo OFF, profiling
  OFF, no nudging) attach by design. *(official)*
- **CSAM:** 18 U.S.C. 2258A imposes **no duty to scan**; reporting only on actual knowledge →
  NCMEC; a report = 1-yr quarantined-preservation obligation. EU voluntary-scanning derogation
  **expired 3 Apr 2026**. Scope for a private *store* (vs messaging) is unsettled. *(official + analysis)*
- **Competitor specifics:** FamilyAlbum non-searchable albums; Apple resists removing a *child*
  account; Google Photos partner-sharing is 1-to-1 (not a circle). *(official)*
- **Legal-precision fix:** under GDPR the **operator** is normally the controller, not the parent —
  frame as "single accountable guardian per child."

## Q4 — Continuous-capture habit & revisit engagement
**Headline:** Capture is driven by killing first-event friction and matching the right **Fogg
prompt** to motivation/ability; revisit is driven by **resurfacing** — but for a child-memory
vault the variable reward must be **opt-IN with per-person/per-date exclusion**, never a guilt
streak, and the durability story is as load-bearing as the habit loop.

- **Fogg prompt taxonomy** — Facilitator / Signal / Spark; a generic daily notification is the
  wrong instrument for a motivated-but-busy parent. *(official)*
- **Resurfacing safety** — automated resurfacing can retraumatize abuse survivors; standard is
  **opt-IN** + accessible, trauma-sensitive exclusion (burden off the victim). Google Photos
  "Show less" vs "Blocked" + hide-date is the control-surface benchmark. *(analysis)*
- **Reject streak/guilt mechanics** for a legacy product (now source-backed). *(analysis, tier-3)*
- **Resurfacing scale:** Facebook On This Day ~60M daily / 155M subscribers (re-cite to Mar 2016
  TechCrunch; ~10 yrs stale). Timehop lost Instagram API access Dec 2024 — HTN *owns* its source
  memories → stronger moat. *(analysis)*
- **Benchmarks:** strong ~30–40% D1 / 10–15% D7 / 5–8% D30; medians ~25/8/4% — blended,
  cross-category, NOT a fair bar for a decades-horizon legacy app. *(analysis)*
- **Refuted:** the "15-min TTFV / 2–3× aha-retention" figures were not in source — only the
  qualitative TTFV-as-activation-lever point survives.

## Cross-question through-line
**SafeBeyond's death** surfaced independently in all four questions as the category's
sharpest data point: a funded, insurer-partnered, emotionally-compelling posthumous-delivery
vendor that ceased operations in ~8 years. It ties together Q1 (handoff), Q2 (vendor-survival
durability), Q3 (decades-promise honesty), and Q4 (engagement is worthless if the vault dies).
The recurring HTN implication: **a standards-based, family-controlled export + an explicit
company-failure / escrow / inheritance-survivability story** is the load-bearing feature behind
the entire "decades" promise.
