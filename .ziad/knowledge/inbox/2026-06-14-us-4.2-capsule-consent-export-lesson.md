# Lesson — Future Capsules can slip the event-scoped consent/export/durability guards

## What fired
Reviewing US-4.2 (date-only Future Capsules). The story is strong on the *named*
catastrophic risk (timing: unlocks on date, never early, gated via clock change),
correctly defers the death-trigger (G9), and is content-blind (G4). But three
un-backfillable / decades guards that the convention writes against **"events"**
are silent for **capsules**, because a capsule is created via its own Add-menu
path (J6), not the US-1.1 event-creation flow:

1. **Legacy consent (G5, US-4.1, H-D2/RUFADAA Tier-1 content-consent gate).**
   US-4.1 captures consent *atomically with an event/circle*. A sealed capsule is
   the single most legacy-critical content (it is *designed* to outlive the moment,
   often the owner). If no consent flag is captured at seal, heir release is
   foreclosed un-backfillably for exactly the content the promise is about.

2. **Export (G5, US-1.4).** US-1.4's payload is scoped to "every event they own."
   A sealed/"locked" capsule may be (a) not modeled as an event and (b) treated as
   un-exportable *because* it is locked — either path forecloses portability for
   capsule content. The posthumous-delivery reference's "in-app-only trap": a
   capsule the dead owner could never export is not covered by the durability/export
   guard at all.

3. **Decades durability ≠ upload integrity (H-C1/H-C4, media-durability fixity).**
   The story applies durability only at upload ("no optimistic confirm"). A capsule
   locked for up to ~18 years needs *lifetime* fixity (periodic re-verification),
   not just ingest integrity.

Plus a trust gap: the "recipient no longer in circle at unlock" case is named in
Edge cases but has **no AC and no defined outcome** — over a multi-year window this
is the *expected* case, and an undefined outcome is the H-D4 silent-miss risk.

## Durable heuristic candidate (promote if it fires 3+ times)
**H-D6 — A new content TYPE must re-inherit every cross-content guard.** When a
feature introduces a content type outside the standard event-creation flow
(capsules, imports, voice-only, etc.), re-verify it carries: per-item legacy
consent (G5/US-4.1), open-format export inclusion (G5/US-1.4), the durability +
fixity baseline, and privacy-circle enforcement — none of these are inherited for
free just because they exist for "events." Fail (any guard silent for the new
type) → NEEDS CLARIFICATION / NEEDS REDESIGN by foreclosure severity.
