# FamilyAlbum / Mitene — Competitor Profile (VERIFIED)

The **largest-traction** private family memory app in the set, and the cleanest
privacy/ads posture among the scaled players. Owned by MIXI (Japanese public co).

## What it does (analyst workflow it enables)
Free, invite-only family photo/video album ("Mitene" in Japan): only invited
family can view; "No followers. No strangers." Auto-organizes by date, generates a
monthly "1-Second Movie," and offers unlimited free storage with device-loss recovery.

## Core design pattern (not the feature surface)
**Free unlimited storage as the acquisition wedge**, monetized on the edges
(prints, premium upload tools, stickers) rather than on the core memory archive.
Like Tinybeans, the habit comes from the **family-share loop**, not from timeline
navigation. The "1-Second Movie" is a resurfacing/playback retention hook (HTN **H-B4**).

## Verified facts
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, web (premium adds computer/web upload). `official`
- **Traction:** App Store **4.9★ / 357K ratings**; Google Play **10M+ installs**;
  surpassed 10M users (Mar 2021), "18M+ users" cited later. `official` / `analysis` (dated).
- **Pricing:** **Free, unlimited storage.** Premium $5.99/mo / $59/yr; Premium Pro
  $10.99/mo / $109/yr; Stickers $3.99. `official`
- **HTN value map:** PRESERVE strong (unlimited free + device-loss recovery). NAVIGATE
  strong (date auto-org, 1-Second Movie). SHARE strong as UI (strict invite-only).
  **LEGACY absent.**

## What HTN should benchmark against
- **Free unlimited storage** as the table-stakes expectation it sets for "preserve."
- Strict invite-only model with a clean "we don't sell your data, **even anonymized**"
  stance — a stronger trust posture than Tinybeans to match or beat.
- Explicit under-13 / parental-consent statement + immediate-deletion commitment
  (stronger child posture than Tinybeans, though doesn't cite COPPA by name).

## Known gaps (HTN's openings)
- **Media-URL protection UNVERIFIED.** Cookies "connect people to the right album after
  invitation" (invite/session-based), but object-level URL auth is undocumented —
  same H-A1 gap as the rest of the cluster.
- GDPR/CCPA named-statute sections **UNVERIFIED** on the English page.
- **No life timeline, no Legacy.** Child/family-album frame; no cross-generational handoff.

## Analyst takeaway
FamilyAlbum sets the hardest bar on **Preserve** (free unlimited + recovery) and a
cleaner trust narrative than Tinybeans — but it is still a UI-circle child-photo album
with no navigable life timeline and no inheritance. HTN cannot win on "free storage"
alone; it wins on the **unified timeline + enforced privacy + legacy** combination
FamilyAlbum does not attempt.
