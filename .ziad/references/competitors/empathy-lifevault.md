# Empathy — LifeVault — Competitor Profile (VERIFIED)

The **funded heavyweight** of the legacy/estate segment. Matters less for feature
overlap than for **what it does to the competitive bar and pricing** of "legacy."

## What it does (analyst workflow it enables)
Empathy is an end-of-life support platform (bereavement + estate administration);
**LifeVault** is its pre-loss planning vault: wills, advance directives, funeral
preferences, personal messages, executor assignment, and secure legal-document
storage. The strategic move is **distribution through insurers**, not direct-to-
consumer app-store competition.

## Core design pattern (not the feature surface)
**Legacy-as-an-insurance-benefit, not a consumer app.** By embedding LifeVault into
Aflac / New York Life / Voya policies, Empathy reaches millions of families who would
never download a "digital legacy app." This reframes the segment: the legacy value
HTN treats as a product feature is, for the funded leader, a **bundled benefit given
away free at massive scale.**

## Verified facts
- **Funding:** **~$162M total** — **$72M Series C (2025)**, prior $47M Series B (2024)
  with reported 300% revenue growth. `analysis` (Calcalist/FinTech Global, multi-source).
- **Distribution/traction:** insurer partners Aflac, New York Life, Voya;
  **~7M people gained access in 4 months.** `analysis` / Aflac IR.
- **HTN value map:** LEGACY strong (will/directive/executor/legal-doc) + SHARE-with-
  trust (executor permissions). PRESERVE thin (no multimedia memory archive). NAVIGATE
  absent. This is the **legal/estate flavor** of legacy, not a memory timeline.

## What HTN should benchmark against
- **The free-baseline pricing pressure** Empathy + platform-native tools (Apple/Google/
  Facebook) jointly create: HTN cannot charge for "posthumous delivery" as a headline —
  it is commoditized and increasingly free.
- Insurer/employer distribution as a channel HTN's legacy value could ride later.

## Known gaps (HTN's openings)
- **No multimedia life archive, no navigable timeline.** It administers death; it does
  not preserve or let heirs *re-experience* a life.
- **No continuous-capture habit** — it's used around a life event, not weekly.

## Primary source for updates
empathy.com; insurer IR/press for distribution scale; Crunchbase for rounds.

## Analyst takeaway
Empathy proves the legacy/estate segment is **well-capitalized and going free via
insurers** — which kills "posthumous delivery" as a HTN differentiator and pressures
any legacy pricing. HTN's legacy value only defends itself when it is **inseparable
from a preserved, navigable, inheritable timeline** — the substrate Empathy entirely lacks.
