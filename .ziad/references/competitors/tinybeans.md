# Tinybeans — Competitor Profile (VERIFIED)

**The category leader for the recurring family-memory habit** — and the single
strongest piece of evidence that HTN's "continuous capture" assumption is
*contested at scale*, not white space.

## What it does (analyst workflow it enables)
Private, invite-only family photo/video journal. A parent captures a child's
photos/videos daily, shares them with an invited inner circle (grandparents,
co-parent), and tracks 300+ developmental milestones — explicitly as the
*private alternative* to posting children to public social media.

## Core design pattern (not the feature surface)
**Recurring capture is driven by an external, emotional trigger** (the child's
daily growth + grandparents waiting to see updates), not by the user choosing to
open a "timeline." The social-pull-to-share is what creates the habit — exactly
the mechanism HTN's review heuristic **H-B2** ("external trigger required; memory
is not a trigger") predicts is necessary. This is why Tinybeans has a habit and
the Navigate-pure timeline apps (Lifely, Timelined) do not.

## Verified facts
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, web. `official`
- **Traction:** App Store **4.9★ / 104K ratings**; Google Play **500K+ installs**;
  markets "millions of parents." `official` (rating counts) / `analysis` (user count).
- **Pricing:** Free = 20 uploads/mo, ~5GB, ads. **Tinybeans+ $7.99/mo or $74.99/yr**
  (unlimited, 200GB, ad-free, 14-day trial). Grandfathered tiers $4.49/$39.99. `official`
- **HTN value map:** PRESERVE strong (200GB premium). NAVIGATE strong (date journal,
  milestones, on-this-day). SHARE strong as UI (invite-only inner circle).
  **LEGACY absent** — no inheritance/succession.

## What HTN should benchmark against
- Frictionless daily capture + grandparent share loop → the habit engine.
- "On this day" resurfacing as the retention mechanic (ties to **H-B4**).
- Polished milestone/journal UX as the consumer expectation bar.

## Known gaps (HTN's openings)
- **Privacy is UI-circle only.** No published statement that shared media URLs are
  auth-gated vs. unauthenticated/guessable — **UNVERIFIED** at the object level. Fires
  HTN heuristic **H-A1** as the wedge.
- **Behavioral-data tension:** CCPA disclosure admits sharing identifiers + internet
  activity + commercial info for **targeted advertising** on free tier; cookies enable
  interest-based ads — conflicts with its Play "doesn't share data" self-attestation.
  (Photo *content* stays private; *behavioral* data does not.)
- **No COPPA statement at all**, despite being entirely child-focused — a notable
  child-safety gap (HTN heuristic **H-A4**).
- **No timeline-of-a-whole-life and no Legacy.** Child-journal frame ages out; nothing
  carries the archive across generations.

## Analyst takeaway
Tinybeans proves the recurring family-memory habit is real and *winnable at scale* —
but only as child photo-journaling, with privacy enforced at the UI not the URL, and
zero legacy. HTN's defensible difference is **the same habit bound to a navigable,
privacy-enforced, inheritable life timeline** — not "another way to share baby photos."
